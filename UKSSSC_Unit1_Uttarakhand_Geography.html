Key State Facts

  • Formed: 9 November 2000 (carved from Uttar Pradesh) as the 27th state
  • Previously called Uttaranchal; renamed Uttarakhand in January 2007
  • Devbhoomi (Land of Gods) — 86% mountainous, 65% forested
  • Divided into 2 divisions: Garhwal (west) and Kumaon (east)
  • Total 13 districts
  • Winter Capital: Dehradun  |  Summer Capital: Bhararisain  |  Judicial Capital: Nainital
💡 Exam Tip: The state was the 27th state, formed on 9 Nov 2000. The name "Uttarakhand" = Sanskrit "Uttara" (North) + "Khand" (part).

🏔️ Mountain Ranges & Physiographic Zones

Uttarakhand is divided into 3 main physiographic zones running NW–SE:

ZoneLocal NameAltitudeKey Features
Greater Himalayas (North)Himadri4,500–7,817 mHighest peaks, glaciers; in 6 districts (Pithoragarh, Uttarkashi, Tehri, Rudraprayag, Chamoli, Bageshwar)
Lesser Himalayas / Middle HimalayasHimachal1,200–4,500 mForested hills, deep valleys; width 70–100 km; copper, graphite, gypsum, magnesite found here; in 9 districts
Shiwaliks / Outer Himalayas (South)—600–1,200 mFoothills; dense forests; gives way to Terai-Bhabar plains

Plains (Southernmost)

  • Bhabar belt: Porous gravel & pebbles deposited by Himalayan streams at Shiwalik base; largely infertile
  • Terai belt: Dense forests, rich biodiversity, marshlands, fertile agricultural land; rich clayey loam soil
💡 Exam Tip: The longitudinal valleys between Lesser Himalayas and Shiwaliks are called Duns (e.g., Doon Valley / Dehradun).

⛏️ Natural & Mineral Resources

Main minerals: Magnesite, Gypsum, Limestone, Dolomite, Marble, Copper, Iron ore, Graphite, Gold (placer)
  • Copper ores historically mined in Garhwal division
  • Graphite & gypsum found in Lesser Himalayas region
  • Magnesite found in middle Himalayas
  • State has significant hydroelectric potential due to rivers
  • Over 175 species of aromatic & medicinal plants (major economic resource)
  • Agriculture: 14% land under cultivation; key crops = rice, wheat, fruits (apples)
  • 65% of population depends on agriculture
💡 Exam Tip: Uttarakhand is called "organic state" — known for medicinal herbs, aromatic plants. Hydroelectricity is a key natural resource. Tehri Dam = highest dam in India.

🪨 Soil Resources of Uttarakhand

All soils in Uttarakhand are susceptible to soil erosion due to steep slopes and fragile geology.

Soil TypeLocationCharacteristics
High Altitude Meadow SoilNear snowline (Greater Himalayas)High organic matter, shallow, well-drained; supports alpine grasses
Podzol SoilConiferous forest zonesAcidic, infertile; leached minerals; distinct profile layers
Brown Forest SoilMiddle altitudes (south of Greater Himalayas)Rich in humus, slightly acidic; good for horticulture; shallow, gravelly
Alluvial SoilRiver valleys (Doon Valley), Terai regionFertile, rich in minerals; ideal for rice, wheat, sugarcane; deep & well-drained
Bhabar SoilBhabar belt (Shiwalik base)Coarse, sandy to gravelly, highly porous, largely infertile
Terai SoilSouthern TeraiRich clayey loam with fine sand & humus; well suited for rice & sugarcane
Glacial/Gravel SoilNorthernmost areasRanges from gravel (glacier debris) to stiff clay
Quartzite SoilNainital districtLight, infertile; contains quartz and cysts
Brown/Lime SoilNainital, Mussoorie, ChakrataHigh percentage of lime
💡 Key contrast: Terai soil = most fertile (rice/sugarcane). Bhabar soil = infertile (porous). Alpine meadow soil = high organic matter.
